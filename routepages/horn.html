Overview

The Around the Horn route allows Colorado State University students and faculty to travel quickly around the university. It also extends southbound to connect to the CSU Veterinary school.

It was opened in Summer 2014 as a part of a $760,000 collaboration between transfort and CSU to enable student transit; $500,000 came from the university and $260,000 from ASCSU.

The route has been fare-free since it opened, with the project also bringing a fare-free period for students riding the MAX BRT route and long-delayed plans for a Foothills shuttle.
